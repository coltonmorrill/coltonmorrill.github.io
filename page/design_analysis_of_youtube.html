YouTube.com

Design Principles

Proximity — Caleb

In this screenshot you can see that proximity makes the site of YouTube work. You can easily tell the relationship of each video. The title, channel image, channel name, views, and picture of the video all sit next to each other. You can tell they are all in relation to each other, but you can also tell the difference inside each information as well. You can see this in the circled parts above.

Alignment — Caleb

YouTube’s alignment is very simple. As you can see by the line I drew, this site has a simple alignment, but it helps in many ways. All the channel profile pictures are lined up and your eye can tell that it is. The same goes for the title of the video and the author. All the way down on this site these items are perfectly aligned, and it makes for a nice to look at website.

Repetition — Kimberlee

There are many different elements that YouTube is repeating for the sake of keeping their page’s connected, two of them are the navigation bar and the colors they use. All except for the page to view a video, the red rectangle on the top of both screenshots stays in the format on the page consistently. This not only helps the viewer navigate through the many pages of YouTube, but also shows the viewer that they are still using the same website. Even if the red bar doesn’t appear when you’re watching a video, the YouTube symbol is consistently in the same spot, just on a different background. Another element that is repeating is the red, white, and black color scheme. Seeing these colors consistency used for the text and background of the many pages of YouTube, also helps the viewer see the connection between pages.

Typography — Colton

The font used on YouTube is Roboto. This is a font created by Google for their android operating system. This is a Sans-Serif font that is easy to read as a heading and as paragraph text. This is a more modern looking font that really compliments the clean look of YouTubes website.

Site Purpose Statement

YouTube is a website where creator and viewer alike can share and watch different kinds of videos from all around the world.

Target Audience

  • Age: 18-34
  • Occupation: Student (High School - College)
  • Income: $0 - $5,000
  • Other: They are looking for a break from life, help or guides to do things, and they are looking for entertainment
